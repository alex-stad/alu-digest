South32 Places Mozal Aluminium Smelter Into Care and Maintenance After Power Deal Impasse

Euranimi Petitions for CBAM Declaration Deadline Extension to December 2027

Euranimi petitioned DG TAXUD to extend the CBAM emissions-declaration deadline for 2026 imports from 30 September to 31 December 2027, citing that non-EU installations can only be verified from January 2027 onward. Without the extension, default emission values could inflate CBAM costs to EUR 600-800/t vs EUR 0-100/t with verified data — a gap that disproportionately hits SME importers and could distort competitive dynamics for European rollers sourcing primary metal.
