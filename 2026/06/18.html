Cold mill at Bay Minette to be commissioned next quarter as project cost reaches $5B

Novelis' Bay Minette, Alabama greenfield plant is on track to commission its cold mill in Q3 2026, with total project cost now estimated at $5 billion, up from the original $4.1 billion figure. The 600 kt/year facility, capable of processing the equivalent of 17 billion used beverage cans annually, remains on schedule for broader commissioning in the second half of 2026.

Aluminium can shortage hits Indian beverage makers during peak summer demand

Indian beverage companies are facing an aluminium can sheet shortage at the peak of summer demand, as supply chain disruptions linked to Middle East tensions tighten availability from Gulf-origin primary producers.

Novelis angle

Can sheet tightness across Asian and Middle Eastern markets signals continued demand-side pressure on Novelis's beverage packaging segment — its largest end market at ~60% of shipments — and reinforces the strategic value of Novelis's closed-loop recycled content supply.

Trade & Regulation 16 Jun #3

Strait of Hormuz closure fuels European, US aluminium supply concerns; regional premiums surge

Three months of Strait of Hormuz closure following US-Israeli strikes on Iran has driven European aluminium billet premiums to $560–600/t and briefly pushed LME aluminium above $3,700/t; a US-Iran framework agreement is expected to allow the strait to reopen around 19 June.

Novelis angle

Normalisation of European P1020 premiums post-reopening would ease input cost pressure on Novelis's ten European plants, which source significant primary aluminium volumes from Gulf producers including EGA.
