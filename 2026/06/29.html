Trade & Regulation 28 Jun #2

EU delays aluminium scrap export curbs till September: will India seize the opportunity?

The European Union has postponed implementation of its aluminium scrap export restriction measures until September 2026, pushing back from the original spring timeline. India — which sources approximately 80% of its domestic scrap demand through imports and was the largest EU scrap importer throughout 2025 and Q1 2026 — is evaluating whether to accelerate procurement before the curbs take effect.

Novelis angle

The three-month delay extends current trade flows; once September restrictions take effect, EU-domiciled recyclers benefit from a tighter domestic scrap market that reduces feedstock competition from non-EU secondary producers — strengthening the economics of Novelis's high-recycled-content European mills.

China's aluminium scrap sector squeezed by tax crackdown, shrinking aluminium scrap margins
