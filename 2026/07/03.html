Trade & Regulation 2 Jul #1

European Aluminium calls for indirect ban on Russian aluminium imports

European Aluminium launched its #NoSecondPassport campaign calling for an enforceable indirect ban on Russian primary aluminium in the EU's next sanctions package, due for approval on 15 July 2026. The campaign highlights a loophole whereby Russian metal processed in third countries—Turkey being the primary route, sourcing ~20% of its primary aluminium from Russia while supplying ~55% of EU extruded aluminium imports—re-enters the EU market as semi-finished products, sustaining around $10bn in annual Russian export revenues.

Novelis angle

Closing this loophole would reduce competitive pressure on European FRP producers from products made with sanctioned Russian metal, strengthening relative demand for low-carbon, closed-loop aluminium where Novelis holds a structural advantage.

Trade & Regulation 3 Jul #2

US Customs to auction 12,000 tonnes of seized aluminium imported from China

US Customs and Border Protection will hold a final public auction of approximately 12,000 tonnes of 6000-series extruded aluminium profiles on 23 July 2026 in Riverside, California, closing a nearly decade-long anti-dumping investigation into imports linked to China Zhongwang Holdings. The material—stored across more than 600 shipping containers—was seized in 2017 after being imported as non-functional aluminium pallets to circumvent US tariffs on finished aluminium products.

Industry News 2 Jul #3

EGA reports ahead-of-schedule progress in Al Taweelah aluminium smelter restart, targets pre-incident production within a year

Emirates Global Aluminium confirmed on 2 July that 89 of Al Taweelah's 1,262 reduction cells are back in operation following the 28 March attack, with bath cleaning approximately 90% complete and frozen metal cleared from over 20% of cells. EGA targets a return to pre-incident output within a year, with recovery running ahead of its internal schedule; first alumina production from the Al Taweelah refinery is expected early in Q3 2026.

Novelis angle

Faster-than-expected EGA recovery signals a gradual easing of the P1020 supply tightness that drove European duty-paid premium spikes in Q2 2026, improving metal availability for Novelis's European rolling mills over the next 12 months.

Industry News 1 Jul #4

Alcoa to acquire South32's bauxite, alumina and aluminium assets for $4.1bn

Alcoa announced a definitive agreement on 30 June to acquire South32's bauxite, alumina and aluminium smelting operations in Australia, South Africa and Brazil for $3.1bn cash plus ~17m new shares, totalling $4.1bn upfront with a $750m contingent value right linked to alumina and aluminium prices through 2030. The deal, targeting H1 2027 close, would make Alcoa the world's second-largest primary aluminium producer at approximately 3.4Mt/yr of combined smelting capacity.

Novelis angle

A more vertically integrated Alcoa at this scale could reshape long-run P1020 supply dynamics globally; further consolidation of the primary sector may affect metal sourcing costs and availability for European FRP producers including Novelis as the deal closes in 2027.
