Competitors 7 Jul #1

Speira label targets reliable carbon performance

Speira has launched VALIS, a verified low-carbon aluminium label for products with a CO₂ footprint independently certified below 4 tCO₂e/t, backed by Environmental Product Declarations. The label initially covers ORBIS can sheet with high recycled content and RIVOS low-carbon primary aluminium.

Novelis angle

Speira’s VALIS certification raises the bar for documented low-carbon credentials in European can sheet, increasing pressure on Novelis to accelerate third-party verification of its recycled-content products.

Trade & Regulation 13 Jul #2

ENVI vote leaves key aluminium loopholes unresolved in CBAM review

The European Parliament’s ENVI Committee voted on 6 July to expand CBAM to downstream aluminium products but left key loopholes unresolved, including the post-consumer scrap threshold, a 50-tonne mass-based exemption, and a pathway allowing Russian aluminium to enter the EU via third-country processing. A full Parliament plenary vote is expected in September 2026.

Novelis angle

Unresolved CBAM loopholes allow continued entry of carbon-intensive aluminium into the EU market, undermining the competitive advantage of Novelis’s low-carbon FRP operations.

Industry News 22 Jul #3

Middle East situation volatile and uncertain, aluminium prices consolidate and adjust

Following the collapse of the Iran-US ceasefire and seven commercial vessel strikes in the Strait of Hormuz, Gulf smelters EGA, ALBA, and Qatalum — representing ~9% of global P1020 supply — face operational disruption. LME aluminium 3-month is at $3,159/t after bouncing from a four-month low of $3,085/t, with LME stocks at ~285,000 t, the lowest since 2022 and down 43% year-to-date.

Novelis angle

Stalled Gulf supply from EGA, ALBA, and Qatalum tightens European P1020 availability and may further elevate ECDP premiums for Novelis’s 10 European plants.
