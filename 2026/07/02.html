End Markets 1 Jul #1

Rising copper prices accelerate shift towards aluminium wiring in automotive industry

Ferrari and BMW are expanding aluminium electrical wiring across new vehicle models as the copper-to-aluminium price ratio climbs above 4.2×; at roughly one-quarter the cost of copper and 3.3 times lighter, aluminium offers a compelling economic case despite carrying only 61% of copper's conductivity. JPMorgan estimates aluminium wiring could displace around 2% of global annual copper demand in 2026, rising to approximately 6% by 2030.

Novelis angle

Escalating OEM adoption of aluminium across additional vehicle sub-systems reinforces the structural long-term demand trajectory for Novelis's automotive flat-rolled and BIW sheet business, as OEM material commitment to aluminium deepens beyond body panels.

Trade & Regulation 1 Jul #2

EU backs new tariff rules with protection for aluminium and steel industries

The EU's new steel and aluminium safeguard regulation entered application on 1 July 2026, cutting duty-free steel import quotas 47% to 18.3 million tonnes per year and raising out-of-quota tariffs to 50% from 25%; the regulation also grants the European Commission authority to impose countermeasures on the US if US tariffs on EU steel and aluminium derivative products remain above 15% by 31 December 2026.

Novelis angle

The EU aluminium tariff retaliation clause — operationalised by year-end if US tariffs remain elevated — raises the risk of further EU-US trade friction affecting Novelis's cross-Atlantic supply chain and competitive positioning at Bay Minette.

Aluminum falls to lowest since February as dollar strengthens

LME aluminium fell to approximately $3,095/t on 1 July 2026, its lowest level since February, extending June's 16% month-on-month decline — the steepest monthly drop since 2008; drivers include a stronger US dollar on Federal Reserve hawkish expectations, rising supply expectations following the Hormuz reopening, and increased production in China and Indonesia.
